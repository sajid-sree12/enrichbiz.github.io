FOREWORD: THE CHANGING WORLD 

Technological innovation is changing the world  by disrupting markets, changing the relative importance of resources, and altering the basis of competition. 

 

Nations, especially developing ones can no longer ignore the advancements of technology and the effects that it will have on its freedom, existence, and well-being. The rapid pace at which these changes are occurring are being felt in every strata of society, every nation, every government.

 

These changes are either rewriting  our very concept of life, living, society, government, nation, or have the potential to do so in the very near future.  

 

Either way, it is imperative that no developing nation, especially Indonesia should ignore the strengths of these advances. It should do everything in its power to embrace, motivate and be a part of this ongoing revolution. 

 

One key thing that comes to mind is the pace of change, every other technology gave us time to adopt, adapt and come to terms with it. This pace is accelerating, we no longer have the luxury of time. The world of 2025 may not even faintly resemble anything like it is in 2020.  

 

The other question that is often asked is what if we do not adopt? What will it do to us? The answer is a simple one. Digitalization and automation are global in nature and will control the highway of commerce and everything else. Efficiencies in every market (commodities, services, manufacturing, health, education, transport) will be rewritten by AI dominant countries. These dominant countries will probably find new ways of colonizing the “have nots”. 

 

Note: Digitization is a conversion of data and processes (paper to bits and bytes).

Digitalization is a transformation. Bringing out insight from the digitized world. Digitalization embraces the ability of digital technology to collect data, establish trends and make better business decisions.

 

ABOUT THIS PAPER

Artificial intelligence has become a main driving force for a new round of industrial transformation around the world. Many countries are seizing the opportunity of the AI revolution to promote domestic economic and technological development. 

 

This paper is about reality and the outcomes that we will face if ignore them and outcomes we face if we are not willing to act and prepare for these changes. The later section of this paper also summarizes and opens a debate on how we can assure ourselves a place in this changing world. The challenges that face Indonesia and how we can change our today so that we are not left behind and be equals on the world stage. 

WHAT SHOULD BE OUR STRATEGY

An AI development plan should outline a strategy for science and technology as well as education and other challenges such as retaining talent, advancing fundamental research, and exploring ethical issues. 

 

Indonesia Artificial Intelligence Development Plan should be a blueprint for a complete AI ecosystem for the country and should address the following verticals

 

AREAS OF ATTENTION

1.      Digitization (big-data technologies, cloud, compute power) 

2.      Digitalization and Automation (Artificial intelligence, Robotics)

3.      Communication (IoT, 5G)

4.      Visualization (Augmented Reality, Virtual reality, Mixed Reality)

5.      Transportation (Autonomous vehicles and infrastructure)

6.      Surveillance and security (Drones, Image recognition, Intelligent Cameras, Voice, Face recognition)

7.      Health and well-being (Gene mapping, slicing,  micro-surgery, micro implants, AI based diagnostics)

8.      Banking and finance (Digital currencies, Tokenization, Wallets)

 

TWO TYPES OF TECHNOLOGY EVOLUTION

Technologies evolve over time in which radically superior technologies displace old, inferior ones, making possible order-of-magnitude improvements in productivity and performance.  The new technology can either be:

 

Competence-enhancing, building on existing knowhow and reinforcing existing technologies (5g, Fiber-optic are examples of such technologies, they radically increase communication speeds). Competence-enhancing technologies increase productivity as well as employment.

 

or 

 

Competence-destroying, rendering existing technologies obsolete and making it possible for newcomers to become technologically superior competitors (Autonomous vehicles, Robotics, process automation). Competence-destroying technologies usually have a negative effect on employment.

 

The above should be taken into consideration while working out the Indonesia Artificial Intelligence Development Plan.

 

 

OUR ADVANTAGE

It is also not always an advantage to be in the forefront of technological innovation. Wait and see—then imitate. Later investments in more mature technologies has proven itself in many ways a superior strategy, especially for countries lagging.

Indonesia Artificial Intelligence Development Plan should take this into consideration as well. What should it immediately seek to close the gap, what it can wait and watch? This will be crucial and will need to be given a lot of consideration.

 

THE ACTION PLAN: ESTABLISH, GROW, EXPAND

 

ESTABLISH

In this phase which should happen in the immediate future (starting now) the focus should be

1.      Partnerships: 

a.      With international AI companies

i.      To bring/create solutions for various verticals

ii.     To educate and train local talent

b.      With Local existing Enterprises 

i.      To focus on AI to be at par with other global companies ii. Especially in the field of medicine and healthcare and infrastructure

2.      Education: Create Scholarships, Curriculum

a.      Invite International Educators/talent

3.      Bring awareness to the masses

a.      Create a campaign that highlights the advantages of Digitization and Automation

b.      With the aim to remove FUD (Fear, Uncertainty, Doubt)

 

GROW

Now that we have local talent, established industry verticals, and removed the FUD factor to is time to start Growing - by establishing research and development centers in AI and other Technologies. With the primary aim to

1.      Building Intellectual property

2.      Building Self-sufficiency and independence through local talent

3.      Providing Opportunities for the now Qualified individuals who wish to continue further research in the field of AI

4.      Create Proof of Concept centers where local innovation can be displayed and demonstrated

 

EXPAND

 

Now that we have established, grown the local talent and capabilities it is time to leave the shores of Indonesia into the Global Markets

1.      Provide Incentives for local enterprises to establish themselves in SEA and then into the advanced countries.

2.      Create Agencies that help to brand and market locally created Products/Solutions for various verticals

3.      This can be done in partnerships with well-established companies Indonesia companies like PERTAMINA, Tokopedia

 

BENEFITS TO THE NATION

There are essentially 4 main areas in which Indonesia can benefit by Establishing an AI Centric Technology Development Program.

TECHNOLOGY INDEPENDENCE THROUGH KNOWLEDGE AND SELF-SUFFICIENCY 

1. This is the first achievement. Self-sufficiency brings about an independence to the Nation. “We can do it” and “we can do it better” attitude not only changes the way we look at ourselves but how the world looks at us.

 

TO BE AT PAR WITH OTHER NATIONS

1.      Other nations and companies which want to do business with us now treat us with respect and at par with them.

2.      Having a qualified talent pool ensures that 

i.      We do not overpay

ii.     We are not pressured iii. We are not being sold sub-par, obsolete products, and services

i.        We can thoroughly whet every product/service that we implement

ii.       We can assert that we have the capability to build it ourselves

 

INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

1. IP creates self-reliance and a better exchange program, especially in the areas of

a.      Infrastructure (Roadways, railways, waterways, and airways)

b.      Essential services (power grids, Telecommunication, water supply)

c.       Other services (banking, healthcare)

d.      Security (Land, sea, air)

i.        As other nations develop technologies that can steal from us in many more ways than before and many times faster than before. 

ii.       We can keep ourselves secure and be assured that we have our own people, our own technology – to protect us, our resources, and our borders (physical and logical)

COMPETITIVE EDGE 

1.      As the world becomes more and more  digitalized and automated it will become more and more efficient. 

2.      Nations using technology are getting better  (price, quality, yields – PQY) in palm-oil, rice coffee, tea, sugar.

3.      We can remain competitive if  we ourselves are self-sufficient, self-reliant, and active participants in this fast-changing industrial revolution of AI and other technologies.

CONCLUSION

Indonesia is a blessed nation, with abundant natural resources, rich, fertile, well irrigated  lands. Technology can help maintain this paradise, enhance it, protect it for many more millions of years to come.

It is paramount that the government and the people of Indonesia realize that technology can be its best friend and that ignoring it will make it its worst enemy. It has now come to a point where adoption of technology is not an option, but a must - if Indonesia must survive, thrive, and enjoy its freedoms.

Another key important point to discuss is  “Time”. Do we have the liberty to delay this? The answer is simple. The sooner we start the lesser is the “difference” we will have to cover. The easier it will be to come up to level. The longer we wait the more difficult it will become to catchup. Simply put, the time is “Now”. 

An AI based Technology program promoted and supported by the government and its people will ensure that Indonesia keeps pace with the world and is not taken advantage of, manipulated, or plundered. Its very survival may depend on the direction it takes today.
